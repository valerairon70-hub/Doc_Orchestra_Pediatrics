# NICE NG101 — Iron Deficiency Anaemia (IDA) in Children

## Diagnosis Criteria
- Hb < 11.0 g/dL (age 6–59 months), < 11.5 g/dL (5–11 years)
- Ferritin < 15 µg/L confirms iron deficiency (< 30 µg/L in presence of inflammation)
- MCV < 70 fL in iron deficiency (microcytic)
- MCHC < 30 g/dL (hypochromic)

## First-line Investigations
- Full Blood Count (FBC) with blood film
- Serum ferritin
- CRP (to interpret ferritin in context of inflammation)
- Reticulocyte count

## Second-line (if no response at 4 weeks)
- Haemoglobin electrophoresis (exclude thalassaemia trait)
- Vitamin B12 and folate
- Coeliac screen (anti-tTG IgA)
- Consider bone marrow aspirate if unexplained

## Management
### Iron supplementation
- Ferrous sulfate 3–6 mg/kg/day elemental iron in 2 divided doses
- Give 30 minutes before meals
- Continue for 3 months after Hb normalises (replenish stores)
- Expected Hb rise: 1–2 g/dL per month

### Dietary advice
- Increase iron-rich foods: red meat, lentils, fortified cereals, dark leafy vegetables
- Vitamin C with meals enhances non-haem iron absorption
- Avoid cow's milk > 500 ml/day (binds iron)
- Avoid tea with meals

## Safety netting for parents
- Return if pallor worsens, breathlessness at rest, or rapid heartbeat
- Stools may turn dark — this is normal with iron tablets
- Repeat FBC in 4 weeks to confirm response

## Source: NICE Guideline NG101, 2021
