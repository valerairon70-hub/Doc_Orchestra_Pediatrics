# BMJ Best Practice — Fever in Children

## Assessment
- Temperature > 38°C (axillary or tympanic)
- Traffic light system (NICE CG160):
  - GREEN: Child alert, normal colour, strong cry, moist mucous membranes
  - AMBER: Not responding normally, decreased activity, pallor, dry mucous membranes, prolonged capillary refill
  - RED: Ill-appearing, inconsolable, non-blanching rash, stiff neck, bulging fontanelle, tachycardia, poor perfusion

## Red Flags (immediate referral)
- Age < 3 months: temperature ≥ 38°C → always refer
- Age 3–6 months: temperature ≥ 39°C
- Non-blanching rash (purpura) → suspect meningococcal disease
- Prolonged fever > 5 days → Kawasaki disease, consider specialist review
- Fever with petechiae → blood cultures and IV antibiotics immediately

## Management: Antipyretics
- Paracetamol 15 mg/kg every 4–6 hours (max 4 doses/24h)
- Ibuprofen 5–10 mg/kg every 6–8 hours with food (avoid if < 3 months or dehydrated)
- Do NOT alternate routinely — use one at a time

## Investigations
- FBC, CRP, blood culture if source not identified
- Urine dipstick and MC&S in < 5 years with fever without focus
- CXR if respiratory symptoms

## Safety netting
- Return if: fever > 5 days, worsening, new rash, stiff neck, very unwell
- Ensure adequate oral fluids
- Do NOT use aspirin in children < 16 years

## Source: NICE CG160, BMJ Best Practice Fever in Children 2024
